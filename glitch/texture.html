Distortion Reflection

Distortion, specifically in this class, has been interesting to play around with. Perhaps, my favorite bit we've experienced thus far was in the pixelated module. Finding smaller compositions within a larger image with the added challenge of making them look like something was truly enjoyable. Playing around with distortion in this way was fun and engaging. This isn't the only way we've used distortion in our modules.



Distortion Reflection II

Distortion, as I've learned, isn't only in making an image "poor quality." It can also be used to change an image as a whole. In module three, "Photoshop Masking and Compositing," we used perfect masks to distort original images based on the artist John Baldessari. This involved drawing on top of older images with bright colors and blocking out the silhouettes of figures and or backgrounds.
